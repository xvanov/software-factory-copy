## Findings

1. **The parity bar was met and exceeded**: factory ≥ Claude on gates-pass
   (7/7 vs 5/7) at ~1/12 the cost — the plan's bar was ≥ parity at ≤ 1/5 cost.
2. **Claude's failure mode is verification discipline**: on t3 and t5 it
   delivered polished diffs (rubric 0.88–0.93) whose own new tests were red —
   it declared done without running them to green. The factory can't do this:
   the authoritative post-run pytest + convergence loop forces green before
   review.
3. **The factory's failure mode is review convergence**: t3 and t8 blocked at
   the 6-cycle cap. On t3 the block was CORRECT (real frontend/backend cookie
   name mismatch the gates can't see, independently confirmed by the blind
   judge). Convergence, not capability, is the remaining bottleneck.
4. **Quality gap is real but modest**: Claude's diffs rubric ~0.08 higher on
   average. The factory's cheaper dev (deepseek-v4-pro) writes gate-passing
   but less polished code; the codex reviewer partially compensates.
5. **Subscription limits are an operational tax on the Claude arm**: 4 of 13
   attempts died on usage-window trips, requiring retries — exactly the
   dependence the factory exists to avoid.
6. **Wall-clock favors Claude ~3x** when it works (14 vs 40 min) — the factory
   pays for its retries/review cycles in time, not money.

## Caveats

- N=1 per task/arm for most cells; claude got best-of-2 on four tasks
  (usage-limit reruns) which slightly favors it.
- One-shot `claude -p` is the weakest reasonable Claude arm; arm C
  (subagent planner/coder/verifier) untested.
- Rubric judge (gpt-5.4) is also a factory persona model; blinded, and its
  scores actually favored Claude, so the bias if any ran against the factory.
- Bench cost rows for the factory leaked into the production runs ledger via
  a db_path threading gap in runner._record (production factory was halted,
  so attribution windows are clean). Fix tracked.
